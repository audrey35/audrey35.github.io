About

My name is Audrey Jo and I am currently pursuing a master’s degree in Computer Science at Northeastern University. Previously, I worked as a Geographic Information Systems (GIS) analyst, analyzing satellite imagery to identify patterns of wildfires and measuring evaporation. As a GIS analyst, I began to realize that my passion for programming, rather than GIS, had the power to change the company’s expectations of a GIS analyst and the ability to change the company culture to encourage collaboration. I wanted to become a developer and create a team of experts from different backgrounds, but a common desire to solve a challenging, multi-disciplinary problem. As a GIS analyst, I developed a variety of skills necessary for success in Computer Science including leadership and solving difficult problems as a team, in a timely manner. In my MSCS program, I have programmed in Java and used Spring Boot and Flask to build REST APIs for data stored in MongoDB and SQL databases. I love to learn and gather new information at a measured pace and give myself time and space to ponder and process the information. I believe that my experience as a GIS analyst, in conjunction with the skills I am developing in my MSCS program, will make me a fit for back-end developer position. My current goal is to find a co-op or internship position that will help me gain experience and an understanding of what it is really like to work as a developer.
